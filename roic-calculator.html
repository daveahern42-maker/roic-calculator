What is ROIC?

Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) measures a company's efficiency at allocating capital under its control to profitable investments, indicating how well it generates earnings from its invested capital.

Key Insights

  • Great companies have high ROIC: Look for sustained ROIC > 15%
  • Compare to WACC: ROIC should exceed the cost of capital
  • Trend matters: Improving ROIC shows better capital allocation
  • Industry context: Capital-intensive industries naturally have lower ROIC
  • Quality signal: High ROIC often indicates a competitive moat
